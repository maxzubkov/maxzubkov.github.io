Research & Publications

Current Research: Lagrangian Grassmann Manifolds

In my present project, I am focused on real Lagrangian Grassmann manifolds within finite real Euclidean spaces. Specifically, I am engaged in the removal of subsets comprised of Lagrangian subspaces that are non-transversal to a predetermined, finite collection of vector subspaces in our Euclidean space.

In a particular special case, I have developed a conjecture concerning the topology of the resulting open complement, U. Additionally, I have a conjecture that considers a potential connection of this topology to its complex analogue.

This project also branches into the study of the image of the Principal Minor map. This map engenders an oriented Lagrangian matroid, the different orientations of which serve as a lower bound for the connected components of the initial space above.

Exploring Determinantal Point Processes (DPP)

As real symmetric matrices are primary component in DPP definition, I am engage in a research of determinantal point processes through the framework of algebraic statistics. The first approach was to count the critical points of the log-likelihood function. There was a success to compute them for small-scale models. This approach allowed to disprove a standing conjecture proposed by Brunel, Moitra, Rigollet, and Urschel.

Additional Research Interests: Polynomial Maps and Neural Networks

Beyond my primary project, I maintain a broad interest in studying the image of polynomial maps. This focus has also led me to engage in research related to Deep Polynomial Neural Networks.
